BASIC

LET'S BEGIN

To start creating trap drums, the first step is to choose a suitable DAW (Digital Audio Workstation), such as FL Studio, Ableton Live or Logic Pro X, as they offer intuitive tools for working with beats. Next, it's essential to gather a set of high-quality samples, including kicks, snares, claps, hi-hats and 808s, which are the foundation of trap's signature sound. Adjust your project's BPM to somewhere between 130 and 180, depending on the style you want to produce. From there, you'll be ready to create drum patterns and explore rhythmic variations.

You will need sounds, Download a good quality trap sample pack, containing kicks, snares, claps, hi-hats, and 808s. Adjust the BPM to somewhere between 130 and 180 depending on the style you want. Load your samples onto individual channels or use a sampler to easily control them.

A sample pack is a collection of pre-recorded sounds used in music productions. In the context of trap, these packs often contain the essential elements for building complete tracks, such as drums, melodies, and effects. They are indispensable tools for beginners and professionals, as they speed up the creative process and offer ready-to-use sounds.

CLAP

The clap is a fundamental element in trap drums, especially because it helps to give that characteristic groove and rhythmic marking. Unlike the snare, which can also be used, the clap has a snappier and generally drier sound, which makes it perfect for adding emphasis and clarity to the rhythm.

The clap is usually placed on the third beat of each measure, that is, on the second and fourth beats in a 4/4 bar. As shown in the image to the side, each vertical bar is a measure.

Standard Clap Sound: The trap clap is generally a dry sound, with little reverb, so as not to lose clarity. Can be used alone or layered with the snare to create a fuller sound.

This creates a basic and constant drum pattern, where the kick (usually on the first and third beat) marks the strong beats, while the clap brings rhythmic emphasis to the weak beats.

808

The 808 is, without a doubt, one of the most iconic and essential elements in the production of trap and many other modern genres, such as hip-hop and rap. That deep, powerful sound isn't just bass; it also acts as one of the music's main rhythmic engines, shaping the foundation and feel of a track. Let's understand how the 808 works and how you can use it effectively in trap production.

The 808 in trap is used both to create a melodic base and to provide a deep rhythmic impact. It can be treated as a traditional bass, but its use goes far beyond that, being one of the main responsible for the weight and the "boomboom" sensation in the tracks.

Bass and Deep: The 808 is a sound with large low frequencies, which can be felt as much as or more than heard, especially in high-quality audio systems.

The tuning of the 808 is extremely important. Often, the 808 is tuned to complement the harmony of the song, playing specific notes or creating a melodic effect rather than just serving as background.
